Camelids

The four llamas and two camels are camelids. Camelids tend to be large and are strictly herbivorous. Camelids differ from ruminants in several ways.
They have a three-chambered rather than a four-chambered digestive tract; an upper lip that is split in two with each part separately mobile; and an
isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have log legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still. (from
Wikipedia)
